# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students preparing for the TC4 Economics final exam, especially learners who need to review dense Thai/English economics material under time pressure. They may study on a laptop while doing calculations, or on mobile for quick recall. Their job is to understand the concepts, remember exam-ready terms, practice changed-number calculations, and build enough confidence to answer short written questions.

## Product Purpose

TC4 Economics Sprint is a hybrid study product: a serious interactive review tool with the visual impact of a high-energy exam landing experience. It turns attached TC4 economics PDFs into a structured web review with complete notes, animated graphs, active-recall exercises, flashcards, and calculation drills for market equilibrium, price ceilings, elasticity, social welfare, and market failure.

Success means a student can explain the core concepts in their own words, calculate exam-style answers without memorizing one fixed example, read demand-supply and welfare graphs correctly, and leave the page feeling alert rather than overwhelmed.

## Anti-references

Do not make this feel like a dry PDF dump, a generic SaaS landing page, a childish quiz game, or a dark visual gimmick that sacrifices readability. Avoid decorative effects that distract from formulas, AI-looking identical card grids, overused grid backgrounds when they no longer serve the learning surface, vague motivational copy, and any inaccurate or unverified economics statement.

## Design Principles

1. Product clarity first, brand impact second: the page can feel dramatic, but every visual decision must help reading, recall, or calculation.
2. Practice like the exam changes the numbers: tools should teach procedures, not just reveal one memorized answer.
3. Graphs are instruments, not decoration: every major graph should make points, areas, shifts, or welfare loss easier to reason about.
4. Active recall beats passive scrolling: every chapter should connect to a question, flashcard, fill-in blank, or short answer pattern.
5. Accuracy carries the wow: any correction, formula, or worked example must be clearer and more reliable than the source slide.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for Thai and English text, with generous line height for Thai reading. Keyboard access should work for buttons, tabs, flashcards, and form fields. Motion may be vivid for the default experience, but it must respect reduced-motion preferences and never gate content visibility. Color should not be the only way to distinguish demand, supply, correctness, warnings, or welfare areas; labels and text states must carry the same meaning.
